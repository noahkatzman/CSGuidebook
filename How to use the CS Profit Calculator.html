How to Use the CS Bazaar Profit Calculator

Cultural Survival Profit Team

Open the Calculator →
Step 1 (Enter your products)
1
Choose how many products Use the dropdown at the top left. Pick 1 to 10.
2
Fill in each product block Each product has 4 fields:
  • Name — what you are selling
  • Cost to Make ($) — what one unit costs to produce
  • Sell Price ($) — what you charge customers
  • Qty to Sell — how many units you plan to sell
Step 2 (Enter your costs)
3
Other costs (left card, below products)
  • Travel Cost ($) — gas, flights, hotel to reach the event
  • Labor Cost ($) — total wages for booth staff
4
Additional expenses (right card, optional) Click "+ Add Expense" for packaging, permits, supplies, etc.
5
Click "Calculate" The results panel appears below automatically.
Results Summary (What each number means)
Total Revenue
Money earned if all products sell at your listed prices
Total Costs
All product costs plus travel, labor, and extra expenses
Net Profit / Loss
Revenue minus costs. Green means profit. Red means loss.
Break Even Revenue
Minimum sales needed to cover all your costs
Fixed Overhead
Costs that stay the same no matter how much you sell
Blended Margin %
Average profit margin across all products. 30% or above is a good sign.
Scenario Planner (Bottom of the page)
Set a profit goal
Enter how much profit you want to make, then click "Show Target". The app tells you exactly how much revenue you need and whether your current products can get you there.
Check actual results
Enter your actual total revenue earned, then click "Show P&L". The app shows your estimated costs and whether you are up or down money for the event.
Good to know

Margin % shows your pricing cushion. If it reads low, it may be worth reviewing your prices or material costs to give yourself more room.

Break even is your safety number. Knowing it before the event helps you plan how much you need to sell to at least cover your costs.

More detail gives better results. Including smaller costs like bags, tags, or processing fees makes the numbers more accurate and useful.
